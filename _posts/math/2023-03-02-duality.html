Explicit Duals

Proposition (Convex Duality) Consider the following optimization problem. $$ \begin{array}{ll} \displaystyle \min_x \ & f(x) \\ \text{s.t.} \ & g(x) \le 0, \\ & h(x) = 0, \end{array} $$ Then, its dual problem is given by $$ \begin{array}{ll} \displaystyle \max_{\lambda, \mu} \ & - ( f + \lambda^\top g + \mu^\top h )^*(0) \\ \text{s.t.} \ & \lambda\ge 0, \end{array} $$ which, assuming \( h(x) := Ax - b \), can be re-written as $$ \begin{array}{ll} \displaystyle \max_{\lambda,\mu,u} \ & \displaystyle -f^*(u^0) - \sum_{i=1}^m \lambda_i g_i^*\left( \frac{u^i}{\lambda_i} \right) + b^\top \mu \\ \text{s.t.} \ & \displaystyle \sum_{i=0}^m u^i + A^\top\mu = 0, \\ & \lambda\ge 0 \end{array} $$
Proposition (Conic Duality) Consider the following conic program. $$ \begin{array}[t]{ll} \displaystyle \min_x \ & c^Tx \\ \textrm{s.t. } & Ax - b \in K \\ & x\in \mathbb R^n \end{array} $$ Then, its dual problem is $$ \begin{array}[t]{ll} \displaystyle \max_\lambda \ & b^T\lambda \\ \textrm{s.t. } & A^T\lambda = c \\ & \lambda\in K^*, \end{array} $$ in which \(K^*\) denotes the dual cone of \( K \).
